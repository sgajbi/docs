# 01 - Insurance, Annuities and Protection-Linked Products: Fundamentals and Taxonomy

## 1. What these products are

Insurance and annuity products are contracts issued by an insurance company. They may provide protection, savings, investment exposure, retirement income, estate planning benefits or a combination of these.

A normal investment product answers: **What asset do I own and what is it worth today?**

An insurance product also answers:

- Who is insured?
- Who owns the policy?
- Who pays the premiums?
- Who receives the benefit?
- What event triggers payment?
- What guarantees exist?
- What values are accessible before maturity or death?
- What charges, surrender penalties, exclusions and conditions apply?

This is why insurance cannot be modelled as just another security type.

## 2. Core product families

| Family | Main objective | Typical wealth relevance |
|---|---|---|
| Term life insurance | Pure death protection for fixed period | Protection gap planning, mortgage/family cover |
| Whole life insurance | Lifetime protection with cash value | Protection + estate + long-term savings |
| Endowment policy | Savings plus protection until maturity | Education funding, disciplined savings, maturity planning |
| Universal life | Flexible premium and death benefit with cash value | HNW estate planning, liquidity planning, wealth transfer |
| Variable universal life / ILP | Insurance wrapper with investment-linked fund value | Investment-linked planning, flexible allocation, protection plus funds |
| Participating policy | Policy shares in participating fund surplus via bonuses/dividends | Long-term savings with guaranteed and non-guaranteed elements |
| Annuity | Converts premium/capital into income stream | Retirement income and longevity risk management |
| Riders | Add-on benefits | Critical illness, disability, waiver of premium, accident, long-term care |
| Premium-financed policy | Insurance policy funded partly by loan | HNW leverage, estate liquidity, yield/rate risk management |
| Private placement life insurance | Customized policy wrapper for eligible HNW/UHNW clients | Tax/estate/investment planning, jurisdiction-specific |

## 3. Parties and roles

Insurance products have role complexity that normal securities do not have.

| Role | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Insurer | Company issuing the policy and responsible for contractual benefits |
| Policy owner | Legal owner of the policy; controls surrender, assignment, beneficiary changes where permitted |
| Life assured / insured | Person whose death, survival or health event triggers benefit |
| Annuitant | Person whose lifetime determines annuity payments |
| Beneficiary | Person/entity receiving death or other benefits |
| Premium payer | Person/account funding premiums; may differ from owner |
| Assignee | Party to whom rights are assigned, often a bank for collateral or premium financing |
| Trustee | May hold policy in trust for estate planning |
| Advisor / distributor | Person/entity advising or distributing the product |

Platform implication: policy ownership, insured life and beneficiary are not the same thing. A policy can be owned by a trust, paid by one person, insure another person and pay benefits to multiple beneficiaries.

## 4. Common values and why they differ

| Value | Description | Used for |
|---|---|---|
| Sum assured | Contractual base protection amount | Death/claim planning |
| Death benefit | Amount payable on death, possibly sum assured plus bonuses/account value | Estate and protection reporting |
| Premium paid | Historical money paid into policy | Cashflow, affordability, IRR |
| Cash value | Accumulated value in permanent policies | Wealth reporting and policy loans |
| Account value | Value of ILP/variable policy sub-fund units | Investment-linked reporting |
| Surrender value | Amount payable if policy is terminated early after charges/loans | Liquidity and balance sheet reporting |
| Guaranteed maturity value | Minimum maturity benefit if conditions are met | Planning and guarantee reporting |
| Non-guaranteed projected value | Illustration based on assumptions | Advisory projection, not actual valuation |
| Policy loan value | Amount that can be borrowed against policy | Liquidity and collateral planning |
| Paid-up value | Reduced benefit after stopping premiums under certain policies | Lapse/continuation planning |

## 5. Product design building blocks

Insurance products combine several building blocks:

| Building block | Meaning | Appears in |
|---|---|---|
| Mortality protection | Payment on death | Term, whole life, UL, ILP |
| Morbidity protection | Payment on illness/disability | Riders, critical illness policies |
| Savings accumulation | Cash value or maturity value | Whole life, endowment, participating policies |
| Investment-linked units | Policy value linked to sub-funds | ILP, VUL, variable annuity |
| Guarantee | Guaranteed sum, cash value, payout rate or death benefit | Whole life, annuity, endowment |
| Bonus/dividend | Participating policy surplus distribution | Participating whole life/endowment |
| Surrender option | Policyholder right to terminate for value | Permanent insurance, annuities |
| Policy loan option | Borrow against cash value | Whole life, universal life |
| Annuitization option | Convert value into income stream | Annuities, pension products |
| Riders | Add benefits/waivers | Most policy families |

## 6. Insurance versus investment product

| Dimension | Pure investment | Insurance / annuity product |
|---|---|---|
| Legal form | Security/fund/deposit | Insurance contract |
| Primary value | Market value/NAV | Policy value, account value, surrender value, benefit value |
| Main risk | Market/credit/liquidity | Mortality, morbidity, longevity, insurer credit, surrender, market, fees |
| Cashflows | Trades, coupons, dividends, redemptions | Premiums, charges, withdrawals, claims, maturity, annuity payments |
| Ownership | Investor owns asset/security | Policy owner owns contract rights |
| Beneficiaries | Usually account owner/heirs | Explicit beneficiary designations |
| Valuation | Price/NAV/model | Insurer statement, account value, actuarial guarantee, surrender schedule |
| Reporting | Portfolio holding | Policy contract and benefit summary |

## 7. Wealth management context

Insurance and annuity products are used for:

- protection planning;
- retirement income planning;
- estate liquidity;
- succession planning;
- education funding;
- tax and jurisdiction planning, where applicable;
- disciplined savings;
- HNW legacy planning;
- key-person/business continuity planning;
- policy financing and collateralized lending.

In advisory and portfolio analytics, these products are not just "investments." They often sit at the boundary between financial planning, wealth management, credit, estate planning and client reporting.

## 8. What should be treated carefully

| Topic | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Guaranteed vs non-guaranteed | Clients often misunderstand projected values as guaranteed values |
| Surrender value vs account value | Accessible value can be lower than displayed investment value |
| Premium affordability | Failure to pay may cause lapse or reduced benefits |
| Early termination | Can result in poor outcomes and loss of protection |
| ILP fees | Multiple charges reduce investment returns |
| Policy loans | Can reduce death benefit and cause lapse if unmanaged |
| Annuity liquidity | Income guarantee may come with limited access to capital |
| Insurer credit quality | Guarantees depend on insurer's ability to pay |
| Beneficiary and ownership | Critical for estate planning and reporting |
