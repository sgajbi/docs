# 10 - Source Notes and Further Reading

## Purpose

This file lists useful sources for further reading. It is not exhaustive and should be periodically reviewed because advisory and suitability rules change over time.

## Regulatory and investor-education references

### Monetary Authority of Singapore, MAS

- MAS Guidelines on Fair Dealing - Board and Senior Management Responsibilities for Delivering Fair Dealing Outcomes to Customers.
- MAS Notice FAA-N16 - Recommendations on Investment Products.
- MAS Practice Note on Recommendations on Investment Products.
- MAS FAQs on updates to the Notice on Recommendations on Investment Products.

Useful concepts:

- fair dealing outcomes;
- advisory governance;
- know-your-client process;
- recommendations on investment products;
- documentation and safeguards;
- pre-transaction controls for selected/vulnerable client groups where applicable;
- financial adviser responsibilities.

### FINRA

- FINRA Rule 2111 - Suitability.
- FINRA Suitability key-topic guidance.
- FINRA Regulation Best Interest topic materials.

Useful concepts:

- reasonable-basis suitability;
- customer-specific suitability;
- quantitative suitability;
- investment profile;
- excessive transaction monitoring;
- ability to meet financial commitment.

### SEC

- SEC Regulation Best Interest FAQs and staff bulletins.
- SEC materials on standards of conduct for broker-dealers and investment advisers.

Useful concepts:

- best interest standard;
- account recommendations;
- care obligations;
- conflicts of interest;
- retail customer recommendation context.

### Vanguard research on portfolio rebalancing

- Vanguard educational and research material on rebalancing.

Useful concepts:

- rebalancing as risk-control process;
- calendar-based, threshold-based and hybrid approaches;
- balancing drift, transaction cost and discipline.

### CFA Institute and GIPS materials

- CFA Institute portfolio management, ethics and performance standards materials.
- GIPS standards for fair representation and full disclosure in performance presentation.

Useful concepts:

- portfolio management process;
- client objectives and constraints;
- benchmark and performance governance;
- fair representation and disclosure.
