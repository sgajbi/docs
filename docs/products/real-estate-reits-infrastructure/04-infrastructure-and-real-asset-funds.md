# 04 — Infrastructure and Real-Asset Funds

## 1. What is infrastructure investing?

Infrastructure investing provides exposure to assets that support essential economic and social activity. These assets often have long operating lives, high upfront capital requirements and recurring cashflows.

Examples:

- airports;
- toll roads;
- railways;
- ports;
- power grids;
- water utilities;
- waste management;
- renewable energy;
- pipelines and storage;
- telecom towers;
- fibre networks;
- data centres;
- hospitals, schools and social housing.

## 2. Infrastructure categories

| Category | Examples | Return drivers |
|---|---|---|
| Economic infrastructure | Roads, ports, airports, rail | Traffic volume, tariffs, concessions |
| Utilities | Electricity, gas, water | Regulated returns, demand, capex |
| Energy infrastructure | Pipelines, storage, transmission | Volume, contracts, commodity sensitivity |
| Renewables | Solar, wind, battery storage | Power purchase agreements, merchant prices |
| Digital infrastructure | Towers, fibre, data centres | Tenant contracts, data demand, power availability |
| Social infrastructure | Hospitals, schools, public facilities | Government/PPP contracts |
| Transport | Airports, ports, toll roads | Economic cycle, mobility, trade |

## 3. Listed vs private infrastructure

| Dimension | Listed infrastructure | Private infrastructure |
|---|---|---|
| Wrapper | Listed stock, trust, ETF, fund | Private fund, partnership, co-investment |
| Liquidity | Exchange/fund liquidity | Long lock-up, limited secondary market |
| Valuation | Market price/NAV | Appraisal/DCF/manager NAV |
| Volatility | Higher mark-to-market volatility | Smoother NAV but valuation lag |
| Access | Broadly available | Often qualified/private investors |
| Lifecycle | Trade, distributions, corporate actions | Commitment, calls, distributions, exits |
| Reporting | Daily/monthly | Quarterly/lagged |

## 4. Infrastructure risk types

| Risk | Explanation |
|---|---|
| Regulatory risk | Tariffs, permitted returns or operating rules may change |
| Political risk | Concession, policy, nationalisation or contract risk |
| Construction risk | Cost overruns, delays, completion failure |
| Volume/demand risk | Traffic, usage or demand may be below forecast |
| Counterparty risk | Offtaker, concession grantor or tenant may default |
| Interest-rate risk | Infrastructure is often leveraged and duration-like |
| Inflation risk | Some contracts pass through inflation; others do not |
| Merchant price risk | Power/energy assets may be exposed to market prices |
| Technology risk | Assets can become obsolete or face new competition |
| Environmental risk | Climate, transition and physical asset risks |
| Currency risk | Cross-border income and asset values may fluctuate |
| Liquidity risk | Private funds and direct assets may be hard to sell |

## 5. Infrastructure cashflow models

| Model | Example | Risk profile |
|---|---|---|
| Regulated asset base | Utility earns regulated return | Stable but regulatory-dependent |
| Availability-based payment | Government pays if asset available | Lower demand risk, public-sector risk |
| User-pay/concession | Toll road/airport users pay | Demand/volume risk |
| Contracted offtake | Power purchase agreement | Counterparty and contract risk |
| Merchant exposure | Energy sold at market price | Higher price volatility |
| Tenant lease model | Towers/data centres | Tenant quality, renewal risk |

## 6. Infrastructure fund lifecycle

Similar to private real estate funds:

| Stage | Description |
|---|---|
| Commitment | Investor commits capital |
| Capital calls | Fund draws capital to buy/build assets |
| Construction/development | Projects built or upgraded |
| Operations | Assets generate cashflow |
| Distributions | Income/refinancing/sale proceeds distributed |
| NAV updates | DCF/appraisal-based valuation updates |
| Exit | Sale/listing/refinancing/secondary transaction |
| Wind-up | Fund liquidation and final distribution |

## 7. Infrastructure metrics

| Metric | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Contracted revenue percentage | Share of revenue under contract |
| Average contract life | Revenue visibility |
| Concession remaining life | Remaining economic rights |
| Availability rate | Asset operational availability |
| Capacity factor | Actual output vs potential output for power assets |
| Regulated asset base | Value on which regulated returns are earned |
| EBITDA margin | Operating profitability |
| Debt/EBITDA | Leverage |
| Debt service coverage ratio | Cashflow to service debt |
| Inflation linkage | Share of revenue/cost indexed to inflation |
| Merchant exposure | Share of uncontracted market-price revenue |
| Counterparty concentration | Reliance on top customers/offtakers |

## 8. Infrastructure in advisory and mandates

Infrastructure may be suitable for clients seeking long-term real-asset exposure and income, but the suitability depends on wrapper.

| Client requirement | Listed infrastructure | Private infrastructure |
|---|---|---|
| Daily liquidity | Better | Poor |
| Stable income | Possible but market-sensitive | Possible but fund-specific |
| Low volatility reporting | No; market price moves daily | Smoother NAV but less transparent |
| Long horizon | Useful | Required |
| Small ticket size | Possible through ETF/fund | Often higher minimums |
| Mandate inclusion | Listed alternatives/equity income | Alternatives/private markets bucket |
| Risk control | Market risk + sector risk | Illiquidity + valuation + concentration risk |

## 9. Real-asset fund taxonomy

Real-asset funds may hold infrastructure, real estate, natural resources, timberland, farmland, renewables or commodity-linked assets. They should be classified by both wrapper and exposure.

| Fund type | Asset class | Key modelling requirement |
|---|---|---|
| Listed real asset fund | Fund/equity | NAV or market price + look-through |
| Private real asset fund | Alternatives | Commitment, capital calls, NAV lag |
| Infrastructure debt fund | Private credit/fixed income | Interest, covenants, collateral |
| Renewable energy fund | Infrastructure | Project metrics and power contracts |
| Timber/farmland fund | Natural resources/real assets | Biological growth, harvest, commodity exposure |

## 10. Platform principle

Infrastructure and real assets should not be forced into only “equity” or only “alternative.” They need multi-dimensional classification:

```text
Wrapper: listed security / fund / private fund / direct asset / debt
Exposure: infrastructure / real estate / renewables / utilities / transport / digital infra
Liquidity: daily / periodic / locked-up
Income: contracted / variable / discretionary
Risk: market / appraisal / leverage / regulatory / inflation / FX
```
