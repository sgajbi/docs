# 07 — Advisory, Mandate, Suitability and Client Reporting Deep Dive

## 1. Advisory framing

Real estate and infrastructure can be attractive to wealth clients, but advisors must explain the product wrapper, not just the investment theme.

A client may say: “I want property income.” The advisor must clarify whether the client wants:

- liquid listed REIT exposure;
- diversified REIT fund/ETF exposure;
- private real estate fund exposure;
- direct property exposure;
- real estate debt income;
- infrastructure income;
- capital-protected/structured exposure to a REIT index.

Each has different risk, liquidity, valuation, income and reporting treatment.

## 2. Advisory suitability checklist

| Area | Questions |
|---|---|
| Objective | Income, growth, inflation hedge, diversification, real asset exposure? |
| Time horizon | Daily liquidity needed or multi-year lock-up acceptable? |
| Risk profile | Can client tolerate price volatility, leverage and property-cycle risk? |
| Income dependency | Does client rely on distributions for spending? |
| Liquidity need | Can client handle gates, lock-ups or no secondary market? |
| Concentration | Existing property exposure through residence/business/REITs? |
| Currency | Are asset and distribution currencies aligned to client needs? |
| Tax | Are distributions taxable/withholding affected? |
| Complexity | Does client understand leverage, NAV lag, return of capital and valuation uncertainty? |
| Product wrapper | Listed, private, fund, direct, structured or debt? |
| Manager/sponsor | Quality, track record, governance, conflicts? |
| Leverage | Gearing, refinancing and interest-rate risk acceptable? |
| Scenario risk | What happens if rates rise, occupancy falls or NAV is written down? |

## 3. Common advisory misconceptions

| Misconception | Better explanation |
|---|---|
| “REITs are safe income products.” | REIT distributions can fall and market prices can be volatile. |
| “Property always protects against inflation.” | Inflation pass-through depends on leases, contracts, regulation and financing costs. |
| “Private real estate NAV is stable.” | NAV may be stale/appraisal-based and can be written down later. |
| “High distribution yield is always good.” | High yield may reflect leverage, weak price, unsustainable payout or return of capital. |
| “Infrastructure is low risk.” | It can have regulatory, political, construction, merchant-price and leverage risk. |
| “Listed REITs diversify equity risk.” | Listed REITs may correlate strongly with equities during market stress. |
| “Direct property value is precise.” | Appraisals are estimates and may lag market conditions. |

## 4. DPM / mandate treatment

Real estate and infrastructure exposures should be captured through mandate classification rules.

| Mandate rule area | Example control |
|---|---|
| Asset class limit | Max 15% real estate exposure |
| Alternatives limit | Private real assets included in alternatives cap |
| Liquidity bucket | Listed REITs liquid; private funds illiquid |
| Single product limit | Max 5% in one REIT/fund |
| Manager/sponsor concentration | Max exposure to one manager/sponsor |
| Geography limit | Max 30% emerging-market real assets |
| Currency limit | Limit unhedged foreign property exposure |
| Leverage limit | Exclude highly leveraged REITs/funds or cap exposure |
| Income mandate | Eligible if sustainable distribution history and income classification passes |
| ESG/climate restriction | Exclude certain asset types or require green certification |
| Private markets cap | Include private real estate/infrastructure with unfunded commitments |
| Collateral/margin | Apply eligible collateral haircut and liquidity restrictions |

## 5. Mandate classification examples

| Product | Mandate classification |
|---|---|
| Listed Singapore industrial REIT | Equity / Real Estate / Listed Income |
| Global REIT ETF | Fund / Equity Real Estate / Liquid |
| Private core real estate fund | Alternatives / Private Real Estate / Illiquid |
| Infrastructure debt fund | Fixed Income or Private Credit / Infrastructure Debt |
| Toll road listed stock | Equity / Infrastructure / Listed |
| Direct residential property | Non-custody Real Asset / Illiquid |
| Structured note linked to REIT basket | Structured Product with Real Estate Underlying |

## 6. Advisory risk scenarios

| Scenario | Client impact |
|---|---|
| Interest rates rise | REIT prices may fall, debt cost increases, cap rates rise |
| Occupancy falls | Income and property value may decline |
| Tenant default | Rental income and valuation affected |
| Refinancing at higher cost | Distributable income may fall |
| Distribution cut | Income objective may not be met |
| NAV write-down | Private fund/direct property value falls after lag |
| Redemption gate | Client cannot exit when desired |
| Rights issue | Client must fund more capital or face dilution |
| FX depreciation | Foreign asset income/value falls in client currency |
| Regulatory change | REIT/infrastructure economics altered |
| Climate event | Physical damage, insurance cost or asset impairment |

## 7. Client reporting design

Client reports should avoid presenting real estate and infrastructure as a simple line item without context.

Recommended sections:

| Report section | Content |
|---|---|
| Holdings | Units/NAV, market value, cost, P&L |
| Income | Distributions/rental income, yield, tax, ROC split |
| Allocation | Real estate/infrastructure %, listed/private split |
| Exposure | Property type, geography, currency, manager/sponsor |
| Liquidity | Daily, periodic, locked-up, gated, redemption notice |
| Valuation quality | Price/NAV/appraisal date and source |
| Risk | Gearing, occupancy, tenant concentration, debt maturity |
| Performance | Price return, income return, FX return, IRR for private funds |
| Mandate | Limit usage and breaches |
| Cashflow forecast | Expected distributions, capital calls, debt maturities |

## 8. Portfolio analytics treatment

| Analytics area | Treatment |
|---|---|
| Allocation | Show both wrapper and economic exposure |
| Performance contribution | Separate price/appraisal return and income return |
| Risk | Use listed volatility for listed REITs; scenario/NAV risk for private/direct |
| Concentration | Sector, geography, tenant, sponsor, manager |
| Liquidity | Listed vs gated vs locked-up vs direct property |
| Income | Yield, stability, coverage, recurring/non-recurring split |
| FX | Asset, income and reporting currency effects |
| Leverage | Product/fund/property-level leverage and linked loans |
| ESG/climate | Physical and transition risk where available |

## 9. Buying power and collateral treatment

Real estate-related holdings may be collateral eligible, but treatment differs.

| Asset | Typical collateral treatment |
|---|---|
| Large liquid listed REIT | May be eligible with equity-like haircut |
| Small illiquid REIT | Lower eligibility or excluded |
| Private real estate fund | Often excluded or low LTV due to illiquidity |
| Direct property | May support mortgage/secured lending if appraised and legally pledged |
| Infrastructure fund | Depends on liquidity and manager/asset quality |
| Non-traded REIT | Often restricted due to illiquidity and valuation uncertainty |

For buying power, avoid counting stale or illiquid NAV at full value without haircut.

## 10. Office/practitioner explanation

A strong advisory explanation:

> Real estate and infrastructure products provide exposure to property and operating assets, often with income and diversification objectives. However, the risk depends heavily on the wrapper. Listed REITs trade like equities and can be volatile. Private real estate and infrastructure funds behave like private markets with capital calls, NAV lag and limited liquidity. Direct property requires appraisal, rent, expense and debt modelling. For a wealth platform, we need to capture wrapper, economic exposure, income type, liquidity, leverage, valuation source and mandate eligibility separately.
