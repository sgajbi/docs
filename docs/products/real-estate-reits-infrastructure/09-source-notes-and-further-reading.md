# 09 — Source Notes and Further Reading

## 1. Purpose of this file

This file records source notes used for factual grounding. The rest of the pack is written as educational and implementation-oriented content for wealth-platform, advisory, mandate, analytics and reporting work.

This is not legal, tax, accounting, regulatory or investment advice. Rules differ by jurisdiction, client classification, product wrapper, listing venue, tax status and distribution channel.

## 2. Core source themes

### REIT structure and investor risks

Investor.gov and SEC investor education materials describe REITs as companies that own or finance income-producing real estate and explain different forms of REIT investment, including publicly traded and non-traded REITs. These sources also highlight that REITs have risks, including market risk, liquidity differences and tax/income considerations.

### Non-traded REIT risks

FINRA guidance on unlisted real estate investment programs emphasises investor communication, valuation, distribution and liquidity concerns. Non-traded REITs may not have an active public market, may have limited redemption arrangements and can be difficult to value.

### Singapore REIT investor education

MoneySense Singapore explains that investors should not assume REITs are low risk or that dividend/distribution income is recurring. It recommends reviewing manager quality, sponsor, property portfolio, income characteristics, leverage and risks before investing.

### Singapore leverage framework

MAS announced in November 2024 that a single aggregate leverage limit of 50% applies to all Singapore REITs, with enhanced disclosure requirements and a minimum interest coverage ratio threshold under the revised framework. Always check current MAS/Singapore CIS Code requirements before using this in regulatory design.

### Infrastructure and private real assets

Infrastructure and private real asset material should be supplemented with manager documents, fund offering memoranda, fund administrator reporting, valuation policies, limited partnership agreements, appraisals and local regulatory requirements.

## 4. Further reading list

- Investor.gov: Real Estate Investment Trusts (REITs)
- SEC investor bulletin materials on REITs and publicly traded REITs
- FINRA Regulatory Notice 13-18 on unlisted real estate investment programs
- MoneySense Singapore: Understanding Real Estate Investment Trusts (REITs)
- MAS media release: rationalised leverage requirements and enhanced disclosures for REITs, November 2024
- REIT annual reports and offering documents
- Fund offering memorandum / PPM / LPA for private real estate and infrastructure funds
- Fund administrator capital call and distribution notices
- Property appraisal standards and valuation reports
- GIPS/private-market reporting guidance for performance concepts where relevant
