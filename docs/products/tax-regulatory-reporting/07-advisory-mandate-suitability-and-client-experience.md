# 07 - Advisory, Mandate, Suitability and Client Experience

## 1. Why tax-aware reporting matters for advisory

Advisors need to explain not only product return, but also the client’s after-tax and after-fee experience.

Common advisory questions:

- How much income did the portfolio generate?
- Was the income gross or net of withholding tax?
- Which products created the tax drag?
- Are there unrealised losses that could be harvested where relevant?
- Are there large realised gains from high turnover?
- Is the portfolio generating income in the right currency?
- Are tax forms missing or expired?
- Is the client exposed to US estate/tax/reporting issues through US situs assets?
- Are trust/family-office structures correctly reflected?
- Is the DPM mandate causing unnecessary taxable turnover?

## 2. Advisory use cases

| Use case | Tax-aware data needed |
|---|---|
| Income planning | Gross/net income, frequency, withholding, currency |
| Portfolio review | Realised/unrealised P&L, tax drag, turnover |
| Product recommendation | Expected income, tax/withholding profile, product domicile |
| Rebalancing | Realised gain/loss impact and transaction costs |
| Tax-loss harvesting | Open lots, unrealised losses, wash-sale/local rules where applicable |
| Concentration reduction | Gain/loss impact from selling concentrated position |
| DPM onboarding | Tax preferences and constraints |
| Cross-border client review | Tax residency, reportable jurisdictions, documentation gaps |
| Trust/family office review | Beneficiary/entity-level views and reporting |
| Year-end planning | Income, realised gains, withholding and documentation review |

## 3. Tax-aware advisory is not tax advice

Advisor language should be careful.

Better wording:

> “This report shows gross income, tax withheld and net income based on the information booked in the platform. Please consult your tax adviser for your personal tax filing and treatment.”

Avoid:

> “This gain is definitely not taxable for you.”

Platform should support tax-aware transparency while legal/tax advice remains controlled.

## 4. Mandate management implications

DPM and advisory mandates may include tax-aware constraints.

| Mandate concern | Example control |
|---|---|
| Turnover control | Avoid excessive realised activity |
| Tax-sensitive rebalancing | Prefer low-gain lots where permitted |
| Income objective | Target net distributable income |
| Currency objective | Match after-tax income currency to client spending needs |
| Product restrictions | Avoid high-withholding products for certain clients |
| Domicile restrictions | Prefer certain fund domiciles/wrappers |
| Liquidity rule | Maintain cash for tax payments/capital calls |
| Leverage rule | Ensure margin interest and collateral risk understood |
| Estate/planning rule | Avoid assets that complicate estate planning where applicable |

## 5. Tax-aware performance views

A strong client experience may show multiple return views:

| Performance view | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Gross-of-tax return | Before withholding tax |
| Net-of-withholding return | After source tax deducted |
| Net-of-fee and tax return | Client cash experience after fees/tax |
| Pre-liquidation return | Ignores potential taxes from unrealised gains |
| After-liquidation estimate | Shows estimated effect if positions sold |
| Tax drag | Difference between gross and net tax return |

The platform should label these clearly. Ambiguous performance labels reduce trust.

## 6. Reporting for relationship managers and investment counsellors

Advisor dashboards should highlight:

- missing or expired tax forms;
- high withholding-tax assets;
- tax reclaim opportunities;
- unusually high realised gains/losses;
- concentration with large unrealised gain;
- corporate actions that changed cost basis;
- products with complex tax/reporting treatment;
- CRS/FATCA documentation exceptions;
- entity/trust ownership mismatches;
- accounts where year-end reporting is incomplete.

## 7. Client conversations by product

| Product | Advisory conversation |
|---|---|
| Equity | Dividend tax, source-country withholding, realised gain if sold |
| Bond | Coupon tax, accrued interest, yield net of tax |
| Fund | Distribution components, fund domicile, reinvestment and TER |
| ETF | Distribution WHT, estate/reporting considerations, tracking exposure |
| Note | Coupon classification, issuer risk, early exit and redemption outcome |
| Private market | K-1/tax-pack delay, return of capital, cash call planning |
| REIT | Distribution sustainability and WHT treatment |
| Derivative | Realised P&L timing, margin, exercise/assignment implications |
| FX | Currency gains/losses and reporting currency impact |
| Loan/margin | Interest expense and leverage risk |
| Insurance | Surrender/claim/maturity distinction |
| Trust | Who reports what and who controls/benefits |

## 8. Mandate reporting examples

| Report | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Turnover and realised activity | Shows implementation discipline |
| Net income report | Shows income objective fulfilment |
| Tax drag report | Shows impact of withholding and product domicile |
| Lot-level unrealised gain/loss | Supports rebalancing and harvesting |
| Concentration with unrealised gain | Helps plan staged diversification |
| Cross-border exposure | Shows source-country and reporting exposure |
| Documentation exception report | Identifies forms needed to avoid higher withholding |

## 9. Client experience principles

Tax-aware reporting should be:

- transparent but not overwhelming;
- clearly labelled gross/net;
- consistent across products;
- linked to transaction detail;
- explainable by the advisor;
- honest about provisional/estimated data;
- clear about limitations and not tax advice;
- available at account, portfolio and relationship levels.

## 10. Example client explanation

> “Your portfolio generated USD 50,000 of gross income during the period. USD 8,000 was withheld at source, mainly on foreign dividends, so your net income received was USD 42,000. The report separates tax withheld from market performance so you can see both investment return and tax drag. Some figures may support your tax adviser, but the report is not a tax filing or tax advice.”

## 11. Interview-ready explanation

> “Tax-aware reporting in wealth management means preserving the facts needed to explain income, withholding, realised gains, product events and cross-border reporting obligations. I would not hardcode tax rules inside each product engine. I would book clean product economics, then enrich transactions with tax classifications, withholding records, cost-basis lots, CRS/FATCA reportability and documentation status. For advisory and DPM, this enables gross/net income reporting, tax-drag analysis, turnover controls, tax-sensitive rebalancing, documentation exception management and transparent client statements.”
