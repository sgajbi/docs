    # Advisory, Mandate, Suitability, Governance, Tax and Client Experience

    **Purpose:** professional knowledge-base material for wealth management, private banking, advisory, DPM/mandates, portfolio analytics, reporting, implementation, QA, operations and office knowledge sharing.  
    **Scope:** educational and platform-design reference only. It is not legal, tax, fiduciary or estate-planning advice. Actual client structures must be validated by qualified legal, tax, trust, compliance and jurisdiction specialists.

    ## 1. Advisory framing

For normal products, advisory asks: is the investment suitable for the client? For wealth structures, advisory also asks: is the investment suitable for the **structure purpose, governing document, legal owner, beneficiaries, liquidity needs and authority model**?

A trust with elderly income beneficiaries and minor remainder beneficiaries may require different advice from an individual growth investor, even if both have the same risk profile score.

## 2. Advisory discovery questions

| Area | Questions |
|---|---|
| Purpose | Why does the structure exist: succession, protection, philanthropy, investment pooling, tax, privacy? |
| Legal owner | Who owns the assets and who is bank client? |
| Beneficiaries | Who benefits now and in future? Are there minors/vulnerable beneficiaries? |
| Authority | Who can instruct? Does trustee/protector/board approval apply? |
| Time horizon | Is the wealth multi-generational or near-term distributable? |
| Liquidity | Are distributions, capital calls, taxes, fees, insurance premiums or loans expected? |
| Restrictions | Are there deed, IPS, board or family constitution limits? |
| Leverage | Is borrowing, margin or pledged collateral permitted? |
| Derivatives | Allowed for hedging only, or also investment/speculation? |
| Tax | Which jurisdictions and tax statuses require expert review? |
| Reporting | Who can see what level of detail? |
| Succession | What happens on death/incapacity/change of trustee? |

## 3. Suitability dimensions

| Dimension | Structure-specific consideration |
|---|---|
| Risk tolerance | Trustee/mandate may be more conservative than settlor preference |
| Capacity for loss | Beneficiaries may depend on distributions |
| Knowledge/experience | Trustee and investment committee sophistication matters |
| Investment horizon | Trust may be long-term but distributions may be short-term |
| Liquidity | Need reserve for fees, taxes, capital calls and beneficiary payments |
| Product complexity | Complex products require trustee understanding and documentation |
| Concentration | Family wealth may already be concentrated in operating business/property |
| Currency | Distribution currency and beneficiary residence matter |
| ESG/values | Family constitution or charitable purpose may restrict holdings |
| Leverage | Credit line may create risk for beneficiaries |

## 4. Mandate design for trusts and family offices

A high-quality mandate should include:

- investment objective;
- base currency and reporting currency;
- strategic asset allocation;
- allowed/prohibited product universe;
- liquidity reserve target;
- income/distribution objective;
- maximum issuer/security/manager/custodian concentration;
- maximum illiquid/private market exposure;
- maximum leverage/LTV/collateral use;
- derivatives purpose: hedging only versus investment;
- tax-aware income preferences;
- ESG/values/religious exclusions;
- approval rules for exceptions;
- reporting frequency and recipients;
- review triggers: beneficiary change, death, trustee change, market stress, liquidity shortfall.

## 5. DPM implications

DPM portfolios for structured clients need stronger controls:

| Control | Reason |
|---|---|
| Authority validation before mandate setup | Ensure trustee/director can appoint DPM manager |
| Restriction extraction | Trust deed/IPS must feed mandate rules |
| Beneficiary-aware liquidity | Income/capital needs may differ |
| Look-through risk | Trust/family may hold same exposure elsewhere |
| Leverage permission | DPM should not use margin if prohibited |
| Derivative purpose tracking | Hedge versus investment classification |
| Distribution reserve | Prevent forced sale of illiquid positions |
| Related-party controls | Family operating companies/private deals may create conflicts |
| Mandate review events | Trustee/beneficiary/governance changes can require refresh |

## 6. Reporting knowledge for advisory and client experience

| Report | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Structure overview | Legal owner, account list, role map, mandate summary |
| Consolidated family report | Total wealth across accounts/entities/custodians |
| Trustee investment report | Portfolio, risk, performance, compliance and distributions |
| Beneficiary report | Entitlement, distributions, restricted portfolio detail |
| Investment committee pack | Allocation, performance, risk, liquidity and decisions |
| Estate liquidity report | Cash available for expenses and distributions |
| Family office CIO report | Manager/fund/asset allocation and stress scenarios |
| Distribution report | Income/capital distributions by beneficiary |
| Compliance exception report | Mandate breaches, overrides and approvals |

## 7. Analytics for family office and UHNW reporting

| Analytics | Description |
|---|---|
| Total wealth bridge | Start NAV + contributions + investment return + distributions + expenses |
| Family branch allocation | Wealth by branch, beneficiary or entity |
| Look-through exposure | Underlying exposure across funds, notes, private assets and wrappers |
| Liquidity ladder | Cash, near-cash, saleable assets, lockups, capital calls |
| Income projection | Coupon/dividend/rent/distribution forecast |
| Distribution sustainability | Whether portfolio income/cash can fund beneficiary needs |
| Concentration dashboard | Issuer, bank, manager, sector, country, currency, property, operating business |
| Cross-currency exposure | Reporting currency versus asset/liability currencies |
| Collateral and leverage | LTV, pledged assets, exposure, headroom, margin calls |
| Private asset pacing | Commitments, unfunded, vintage exposure, capital-call forecast |
| Tax-sensitive income | Income/capital classification for tax advisors |

## 8. Governance workflow

Example distribution request workflow:

```text
Beneficiary request -> Family office review -> Trustee decision -> Protector consent if required -> Liquidity check -> Tax/legal check -> Payment approval -> Transaction posting -> Beneficiary report update
```

Example investment exception workflow:

```text
Advisor proposal -> Suitability check -> Trust deed/IPS check -> Mandate breach analysis -> Trustee/investment committee approval -> Trade execution -> Post-trade compliance -> Reporting disclosure
```

## 9. Tax awareness without giving tax advice

Platforms should not make legal/tax conclusions unless rules are formally approved. However, platforms should store and expose tax-relevant attributes:

- tax residency of entities and beneficiaries;
- FATCA/CRS status;
- trust classification;
- estate status;
- tax incentive status for fund/family office vehicles;
- income versus capital classification;
- withholding tax on income;
- reportable jurisdiction flags;
- date-of-death valuation;
- realized gains/losses;
- document references and advisor notes.

## 10. Client experience design

For UHNW/family office clients, good UX is not only transactions. It should support:

- family relationship map;
- entity and account hierarchy;
- beneficiary/distribution dashboard;
- document vault;
- investment committee agenda and approvals;
- consolidated exposure and risk dashboard;
- multi-currency total wealth view;
- liquidity and capital-call calendar;
- family branch reporting;
- exception and mandate-breach workflow;
- advisor narrative explaining what changed.

## 11. Advisory red flags

| Red flag | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Settlor instructs despite trustee legal ownership | Authority risk |
| Beneficiary asks for full report without entitlement | Privacy/confidentiality risk |
| Trust buys high-risk illiquid product while near-term distributions expected | Liquidity/suitability risk |
| Family office requests leverage but trust deed prohibits borrowing | Mandate/legal restriction risk |
| Single issuer/bank dominates family wealth | Concentration/counterparty risk |
| Insurance policy beneficiary conflicts with will | Estate planning mismatch |
| LPA/POA status unclear | Incapacity authority risk |
| Cross-border beneficiaries | Tax/legal/reporting complexity |
| Family operating business used as collateral | Liquidity and conflict risk |
