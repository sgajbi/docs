# 11 - Source Notes and Further Reading

This file lists external sources and standards used as reference points for the pack. The documentation is written as educational and platform-design guidance, not as legal, regulatory or compliance advice.

## Primary governance and AI risk references

| Source | Relevance |
|---|---|
| NIST AI Risk Management Framework 1.0 | Voluntary AI risk-management framework with Govern, Map, Measure and Manage functions; useful for AI lifecycle governance and trustworthiness concepts. |
| NIST AI RMF Generative AI Profile | Companion profile for generative AI risks and actions across the AI lifecycle. |
| ISO/IEC 42001:2023 | AI management system standard for establishing, implementing, maintaining and improving responsible AI governance in organizations. |
| AI Verify Foundation - AI Verify Testing Framework | Practical AI governance testing framework aligned with international principles; includes GenAI considerations and evidence-based process checks. |
| AI Verify Foundation - Project Moonshot | LLM evaluation toolkit for benchmarking and red-teaming LLM applications. |
| OWASP Top 10 for LLM Applications 2025 | Security risks and mitigations for LLM and GenAI applications including prompt injection, sensitive information disclosure, supply chain, data/model poisoning, excessive agency and vector weaknesses. |
| EU Artificial Intelligence Act, Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 | Risk-based legal framework for AI in the EU; useful for understanding global direction on high-risk AI, transparency and GPAI obligations. |
| Singapore PDPC / IMDA AI Governance materials | Singapore's responsible AI and governance approach; useful local/regional context for trusted AI adoption. |

## Wealth / banking concepts connected to AI

| Prior pack | Connection to AI |
|---|---|
| Product governance and APU | AI must consume product eligibility, risk rating, target market and disclosure rules. |
| Client/account/portfolio master | AI must respect client hierarchy, suitability profile, booking centre and entitlements. |
| Portfolio performance and risk | AI can explain official analytics but should not replace calculation engines. |
| Model portfolios and suitability | AI can draft proposals and explain checks, but deterministic suitability/mandate engines should govern decisions. |
| Market/reference data | AI answers depend on source ownership, golden copy, stale price and lineage controls. |
| Data governance and controls | AI needs data quality, lineage, reconciliation and incident controls. |
| Portfolio reporting | AI can generate narratives and exception explanations from approved reporting snapshots. |
| Trade lifecycle and operations | AI agents may support workflow but require tool permissions, approvals and audit. |
| Investment accounting | AI should use official ledger/accounting records for income, P&L, cash and positions. |
