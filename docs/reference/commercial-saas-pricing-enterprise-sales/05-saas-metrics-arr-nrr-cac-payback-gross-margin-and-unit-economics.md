# 05 - SaaS Metrics: ARR, NRR, CAC Payback, Gross Margin and Unit Economics

## 1. Why SaaS metrics matter

SaaS metrics show whether the business can grow sustainably.

For a bank-buyable vendor, metrics also signal:

- commercial stability
- ability to invest in product
- support sustainability
- renewal discipline
- enterprise maturity
- valuation and fundraising readiness

## 2. Core metrics

| Metric | Meaning |
|---|---|
| ARR | Annual recurring revenue |
| MRR | Monthly recurring revenue |
| ACV | Annual contract value |
| TCV | Total contract value over contract term |
| NRR | Net revenue retention including expansion and contraction |
| GRR | Gross revenue retention excluding expansion |
| CAC | Customer acquisition cost |
| CAC payback | Months to recover acquisition cost |
| Gross margin | Revenue minus cost of service delivery |
| Logo retention | Percentage of customers retained |
| Churn | Customer or revenue lost |
| Expansion revenue | Revenue from upsell/cross-sell |
| Pipeline | Potential sales opportunities |
| Win rate | Percentage of opportunities won |
| Sales cycle | Time from opportunity to close |

## 3. ARR

ARR is recurring subscription revenue normalized annually.

Example:

| Contract | Annual recurring fee |
|---|---:|
| Bank A | 500,000 |
| Bank B | 300,000 |
| Bank C | 200,000 |
| Total ARR | 1,000,000 |

Do not include one-time professional services in ARR unless they are recurring managed-service fees.

## 4. ACV versus TCV

| Metric | Example |
|---|---|
| ACV | USD 500k per year |
| TCV | USD 1.5m for three-year term |

A bank may sign a large TCV but revenue recognition, cash collection and delivery obligations may differ.

## 5. NRR

Net revenue retention shows whether existing customers expand.

```text
NRR = (Starting ARR - contraction - churn + expansion) / Starting ARR
```

Example:

| Item | Amount |
|---|---:|
| Starting ARR | 1,000,000 |
| Churn | 50,000 |
| Contraction | 50,000 |
| Expansion | 200,000 |
| Ending retained ARR | 1,100,000 |
| NRR | 110% |

## 6. Gross margin

Gross margin should consider cost to serve:

- cloud hosting
- support team
- data vendors
- AI token/model cost
- managed service operations
- customer-specific infrastructure
- monitoring/observability
- security/compliance operations

```text
Gross Margin = (Revenue - Cost of Goods Sold) / Revenue
```

For enterprise SaaS, heavy customization and dedicated environments can reduce gross margin.

## 7. CAC payback

CAC payback estimates how long it takes to recover sales and marketing cost from gross profit.

```text
CAC Payback Months = CAC / Monthly Gross Profit from Customer
```

Enterprise sales often has long CAC payback because sales cycles are long and due diligence is heavy.

## 8. Revenue recognition

SaaS companies must distinguish:

- booked contract value
- invoiced amount
- cash collected
- revenue recognized
- deferred revenue
- implementation revenue
- subscription revenue

The accounting treatment depends on contract structure and performance obligations.

## 10. Metrics dashboard

A founder dashboard should include:

- ARR
- pipeline
- ACV
- gross margin
- churn risk
- renewal calendar
- NRR
- implementation backlog
- support tickets
- SLA performance
- cloud cost by customer
- AI cost by customer
- sales cycle length
- win/loss reasons

## 11. Key takeaway

SaaS metrics connect product strategy to financial sustainability.
