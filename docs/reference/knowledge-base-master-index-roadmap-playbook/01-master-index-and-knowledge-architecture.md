# 01 - Master Index and Knowledge Architecture

## 1. Knowledge base architecture

The knowledge base is organized into seven layers:

```text
Layer 1 - Product knowledge
Layer 2 - Product lifecycle, transactions and valuation
Layer 3 - Portfolio analytics, reporting and advisory
Layer 4 - Data, accounting, operations and controls
Layer 5 - Platform architecture, cloud, security and delivery
Layer 6 - AI, decision intelligence and wealth-platform strategy
Layer 7 - Commercial, operating model and transformation strategy
```

Each layer builds on the earlier layers.

## 8. How to use the knowledge base

### For learning

Start with products, then lifecycle, then analytics, then architecture.

### For project work

Start with the domain pack closest to the problem, then read the cross-cutting packs for data, reporting, controls and architecture.

### For practitioners

Use the practitioner reference files inside each pack, then use the master practitioner playbook.

### For office knowledge sharing

Use the office playbook and convert sections into short sessions.

### For wealth-platform suite

Use the wealth-platform strategy and commercial packs together with product and platform packs.

## 9. Master rule

Every new pack should answer:

- What is the topic?
- Why does it matter in wealth management?
- What are the product/business concepts?
- What are the lifecycle events?
- How are transactions, positions and cashflows modelled?
- How is valuation/performance/risk/reporting handled?
- What are advisory and mandate implications?
- What are platform, data and control implications?
- What are QA and edge cases?
- What are practitioner-ready explanations?
