# 02 - Study Roadmap by Level, Role and Goal

## 1. Study levels

Use four levels:

| Level | Goal |
|---|---|
| Level 1 - Product literacy | Understand product purpose, risk and basic lifecycle |
| Level 2 - Platform modelling | Understand transaction, position, valuation and data treatment |
| Level 3 - Advisory / analytics mastery | Understand suitability, mandates, performance, risk and reporting |
| Level 4 - Architecture / strategy mastery | Understand platform design, controls, AI, transformation and commercial strategy |

## 2. Beginner path

Recommended order:

1. Bonds
2. Funds
3. Equities
4. Cash, deposits, money market and FX
5. Structured notes
6. Derivatives
7. Private markets
8. Insurance and annuities
9. Loans / Lombard / collateral
10. Real estate / REITs / infrastructure

Objective: become fluent in product behaviour.

## 3. Portfolio analytics path

Recommended order:

1. Investment accounting and ledger
2. Market data and pricing
3. Portfolio performance / attribution / risk / benchmarks
4. Product-specific analytics treatment inside each product pack
5. Tax and regulatory reporting
6. Portfolio reporting and client experience
7. Data governance / lineage

Objective: become strong in performance, P&L, valuation, risk and report correctness.

## 4. Advisory and DPM path

Recommended order:

1. Product governance / APU / target market
2. Model portfolios / rebalancing / suitability
3. Portfolio performance / risk / benchmarks
4. Product packs for major asset classes
5. Client/account/portfolio master
6. Entitlements / advisor workbench
7. AI decision intelligence

Objective: understand how advisors and portfolio managers use the platform.

## 5. Technology architecture path

Recommended order:

## 8. Daily study routine

For each topic:

```text
Read fundamentals
-> read lifecycle and data model
-> write 10 bullet summary
-> create one worked example
-> explain advisory impact
-> explain reporting impact
-> explain platform design
-> prepare practitioner answer
```

## 9. Active recall questions

For every product, answer:

- What is the product?
- Who issues it?
- Who owns it legally?
- What cashflows occur?
- What are lifecycle events?
- What can go wrong?
- How is it valued?
- How is performance calculated?
- How is risk represented?
- What should advisor explain?
- What should report disclose?
- What data is required?
- What controls are needed?

## 10. Key takeaway

Do not study as isolated documents. Study as connected wealth-platform capability.
