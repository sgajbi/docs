# 07 - Wealth Platform, Advisory, Reporting and Client Impact

## 1. Why vendor risk affects client outcomes

In wealth management, vendor failure can directly affect:

- client statements
- portfolio valuations
- advisory proposals
- suitability checks
- DPM mandate monitoring
- product eligibility
- trading workflows
- performance reporting
- risk dashboards
- tax and regulatory reporting
- client trust

Vendor risk is therefore not only technology risk. It is client-outcome risk.

## 2. Product data vendor impact

If product master data is wrong:

- unsuitable product may be shown
- restricted product may be sold
- wrong risk rating may be applied
- liquidity classification may be wrong
- target market may be missed
- disclosure may be absent
- reporting labels may be incorrect

## 3. Market data and pricing vendor impact

If pricing data is wrong:

- market value may be wrong
- P&L may be wrong
- performance may be misstated
- risk and concentration may be distorted
- collateral availability may be incorrect
- margin or buying power may be wrong
- client statement may be challenged

## 4. Reporting vendor impact

If statement/PDF generation fails:

- client reports may be delayed
- regulatory or contractual deadlines may be missed
- advisor cannot prepare reviews
- historical report archive may be incomplete
- client trust may be damaged

## 5. AI vendor impact

If AI output is wrong or uncontrolled:

- advisor may receive unsupported recommendation
- client may receive misleading explanation
- mandate breach may be missed
- suitability issue may be overlooked
- confidential data may leak
- hallucination may be treated as fact
- audit trail may be insufficient

## 6. Advisory controls

Vendor-supported advisory workflows should enforce:

- approved product universe
- client suitability and target market
- product restrictions
- booking-centre eligibility
- mandate constraints
- documentation and disclosure
- human approval
- explanation lineage
- exception handling
- audit trail

## 7. DPM / mandate controls

For discretionary portfolios, vendor systems must not bypass:

- mandate allocation ranges
- prohibited instruments
- concentration limits
- liquidity limits
- leverage rules
- ESG or client restrictions
- benchmark-relative guidelines
- rebalancing tolerances
- portfolio manager approvals

## 8. Reporting controls

Reports using vendor data should show:

- valuation source
- reporting date
- stale or missing price warnings
- estimated values
- unsupported analytics exclusions
- data-quality flags
- restatement status
- benchmark source
- calculation methodology
- disclosures

## 9. Client communication during vendor incidents

When a vendor incident affects clients, communication should be:

- accurate
- timely
- approved
- specific to impact
- not overly technical
- clear on remedial action
- clear on whether values are estimated
- consistent across advisors and channels

## 10. Key takeaway

The vendor control model must map vendor failure to client impact, not only system impact.
