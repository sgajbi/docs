# 10 - Roadmap, Business Case, Operating Model and Go-to-Market

## 1. Roadmap philosophy

Build AI capabilities in layers:

1. knowledge grounding
2. read-only explanation
3. advisor productivity
4. report narrative generation
5. suitability/mandate-aware decision support
6. idea generation and next-best-action
7. controlled agentic workflows
8. client-facing AI
9. semi-automated operations and portfolio workflows with approval

Do not start with the highest-risk use case. Start with use cases that build trust, data quality and reusable platform services.

## 2. 12-month capability roadmap

| Phase | Focus | Key deliverables |
|---|---|---|
| 0 - Foundation | Governance and architecture | AI policy, use-case risk taxonomy, AI gateway, prompt registry, audit model. |
| 1 - Knowledge | Product and methodology assistant | RAG over product KB, methodology docs, source citations, evaluation set. |
| 2 - Advisor productivity | Meeting prep and portfolio review | Meeting brief, portfolio change summary, report narrative draft. |

## 6. Enterprise adoption approach

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| Start with internal users | Advisors, PMs, BAs, ops, QA before clients. |
| Use approved knowledge | Avoid open-ended internet or unapproved sources for regulated workflows. |
| Measure every pilot | Baseline productivity and quality before rollout. |
| Require feedback | Human correction becomes evaluation data. |
| Show evidence | Build trust through citations and lineage. |
| Keep controls visible | Users should see why something was blocked or caveated. |
| Reuse components | Same RAG, prompt, audit and evaluation services across use cases. |

## 7. Go-to-market positioning for a wealth AI platform

Possible positioning:

```text
Wealth AI is a governed decision-intelligence layer for private banks and wealth platforms, helping advisors, portfolio managers and operations teams explain portfolios, generate suitable ideas, prepare client reviews, triage exceptions and produce auditable reporting narratives using controlled RAG, agent workflows and enterprise AI governance.
```

## 8. Market differentiators

| Differentiator | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Wealth-domain depth | Most AI tools lack product lifecycle, mandate and portfolio analytics knowledge. |
| Cross-product coverage | Covers notes, bonds, funds, equities, derivatives, alternatives, lending, insurance and real assets. |
| Analytics integration | Converts performance/risk/attribution outputs into narrative and action. |
| Governance-first design | Better fit for bank procurement and regulated workflows. |
| RAG over curated KB | Reuses your high-quality documentation base. |
| Agentic but controlled | Workflow automation with approval and audit. |
| Bank-buyable architecture | Security, privacy, lineage, observability and testing are first-class. |

## 10. Build-versus-buy framing

Banks may already have enterprise AI platforms. Wealth platform should not compete only as a model wrapper. Wealth platform should provide wealth-specific domain capability that can integrate with a bank's AI platform.

Integration modes:

- use bank-approved model provider
- use bank vector store
- use wealth-domain prompts and evaluations
- use wealth-platform tools and APIs
- use wealth product and analytics knowledge base
- use bank identity/entitlement/audit stack
