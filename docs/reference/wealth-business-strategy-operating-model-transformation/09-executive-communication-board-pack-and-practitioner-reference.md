# 09 - Executive Communication, Board Pack and practitioner reference

## 1. Why executive communication matters

Senior leaders do not need every implementation detail. They need:

- why change is needed
- what business outcome is targeted
- what risks exist
- what investment is required
- what roadmap is proposed
- what value will be delivered
- what decisions are required

## 2. Executive transformation story

A strong narrative:

> Our wealth platform must evolve from fragmented systems and manual controls into a scalable, data-driven, AI-enabled and control-by-design platform. The transformation will improve advisor productivity, reporting trust, DPM scalability, suitability governance, operational resilience and multi-country rollout capability.

## 3. Board-pack structure

| Section | Content |
|---|---|
| Executive summary | decision requested |
| Current-state pain | risks and business constraints |
| Strategic ambition | target outcomes |
| Capability map | what must change |
| Target operating model | future people/process/technology/data/control model |
| Architecture | target platform and integration pattern |
| Roadmap | phased delivery |
| Investment | cost and funding model |
| Benefits | value case and KPIs |
| Risks | key risks and mitigations |
| Decisions | approvals required |

## 4. Senior practitioner answer: transformation

> I approach transformation by first understanding the business outcome, not just the application change. I map the value chain and capabilities, identify current-state gaps, define the target operating model, align the data and architecture model, and then create a phased roadmap with clear ownership, controls and measurable value. In wealth management, I pay special attention to product governance, suitability, DPM mandates, performance continuity, reporting quality, data lineage and country-specific requirements.

## 5. Senior practitioner answer: AI

> I see AI as a decision-intelligence layer across wealth platforms, not just a chatbot. The high-value use cases are advisor preparation, portfolio review explanation, product knowledge, risk and mandate narrative, operations triage and proposal support. But AI must be grounded in approved sources, entitlement-aware, evaluated, auditable and human-supervised, especially where outputs influence advice or client communication.

## 6. Senior practitioner answer: architecture

> My architecture approach is domain-aligned and source-backed. I separate systems of record, calculation engines, workflow services, reporting snapshots and AI orchestration. I prefer clear APIs and event contracts, versioned calculations, immutable audit trails, data-quality controls and explicit ownership. This makes the platform scalable, explainable and easier to operate across countries.

## 7. Senior practitioner answer: reporting

> Client reporting is not just a PDF output. It is the final expression of data quality, product modelling, accounting, valuation, performance, risk and controls. A strong reporting platform needs reproducible snapshots, source lineage, clear exceptions, methodology disclosure, entitlement controls and report archive.

## 8. Senior practitioner answer: DPM

> DPM platforms require model portfolios, mandate rules, drift monitoring, rebalancing, restrictions, benchmark-relative analytics and breach management. The platform must distinguish advice from discretionary action and provide evidence for decisions, approvals and client reporting.

## 9. Executive one-liners

- Strategy without operating model becomes aspiration.
- Operating model without architecture becomes manual work.
- Architecture without data ownership becomes fragile.
- AI without controls becomes risk.
- Reporting without lineage becomes untrusted.
- Transformation without value tracking becomes cost.
- Platform modernization without decommissioning becomes duplication.

## 10. Key takeaway

Senior communication should connect business outcome, platform design, operating controls and measurable value.
