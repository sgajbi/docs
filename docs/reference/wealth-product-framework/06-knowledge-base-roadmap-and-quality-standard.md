# 06 — Knowledge Base Roadmap and Quality Standard

## 1. Purpose

The financial products knowledge base should be built as a durable professional reference, not ad hoc study notes. Each product pack should be consistent enough to support study, project design, advisory understanding, analytics, reporting and implementation.

## 2. Standard structure for each product pack

Recommended structure:

1. Fundamentals and product taxonomy
2. Market structure or product operating model
3. Lifecycle, transactions and position modelling
4. Product-specific complex lifecycle events
5. Data model, valuation, P&L and performance
6. Advisory, mandate, analytics and reporting
7. Platform implementation, controls and QA
8. Glossary, practitioner reference and worked examples

## 3. Quality checklist

Each product pack should cover:

- what the product is;
- how the investor makes or loses money;
- product variants;
- lifecycle;
- transaction types;
- position model;
- valuation model;
- income treatment;
- cost basis and P&L;
- performance treatment;
- risk analytics;
- advisory and suitability;
- mandate/DPM treatment;
- reporting requirements;
- data model;
- controls and reconciliation;
- QA/golden scenarios;
- practitioner explanations;
- worked examples.

## 4. Product Pack Roadmap

Covered product packs now include:

1. notes and structured notes,
2. bonds and fixed income,
3. funds and pooled investment products,
4. equities and market operations,
5. derivatives and overlay products,
6. commodities, precious metals and real assets,
7. cash, deposits, money market and FX,
8. structured products,
9. alternatives and private markets,
10. insurance and annuity products,
11. loans, Lombard lending, margin, collateral and buying power,
12. real estate, REITs and infrastructure products.

Potential future packs:

1. mandates and advisory workflows,
2. client reporting and portfolio review methodology,
3. performance, attribution, risk and concentration methodology,
4. source-data and market-data governance.

## 5. Knowledge base design principle

> A good product knowledge base should teach the product, explain its lifecycle, and help engineers build systems that keep economic truth intact from trade capture to reporting.
