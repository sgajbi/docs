# Technical Strategy, Roadmap, and Platform Direction

## Purpose

This file explains how engineering leaders and architects shape technical strategy and roadmap.

A technical roadmap should connect business capability, platform maturity, quality, reliability, and engineering capacity.

---

## Technical Strategy

Technical strategy answers:

- Where are we going?
- Why does it matter?
- What capabilities must improve?
- What architecture principles guide decisions?
- What should be standardized?
- What should be modernized?
- What risks must be reduced?
- What should be stopped or retired?

---

## Strategy Inputs

Inputs include:

- business roadmap
- product capability gaps
- production incidents
- technical debt
- platform standards
- security findings
- regulatory/control expectations
- team capability
- operational pain
- delivery bottlenecks
- cost and capacity trends

---

## Roadmap Categories

| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| Capability delivery | New product/domain functionality. |
| Platform modernization | Runtime, CI/CD, observability, security, data products. |
| Quality improvement | Test strategy, contracts, refactoring, docs. |
| Reliability | SLOs, alerts, runbooks, resilience, recovery. |
| Security | IAM, secrets, dependency hygiene, sensitive-data controls. |
| Technical debt | Simplification, deprecation, duplicate removal. |
| Developer experience | Templates, local setup, automation, KT. |

---

## Roadmap Principles

A good roadmap should:

- balance feature and foundation work
- be evidence-based
- include risk reduction
- be sequenced
- include measurable outcomes
- show dependencies
- avoid unrealistic big-bang modernization
- create incremental value

---

## Modernization Themes

Common themes:

- service boundary cleanup
- API governance
- data-product maturity
- CI/CD gate promotion
- observability standardization
- runtime/container hardening
- security posture improvement
- test pyramid correction
- documentation consolidation
- operational runbook maturity

---

## Strategy Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Impact |
|---|---|
| Roadmap only feature delivery | Platform quality decays. |
| Modernization with no business link | Hard to fund. |
| Big-bang rewrite | High risk and slow value. |
| No measurable outcomes | Progress subjective. |
| Ignoring incidents | Repeated failures. |
| Strategy kept in leader's head | Team misalignment. |
| Too many priorities | Nothing finishes. |

---

## Review Checklist

- Is technical strategy documented?
- Does it connect to business outcomes?
- Are risks and incidents considered?
- Are foundation items included?
- Are outcomes measurable?
- Are dependencies clear?
- Is sequencing realistic?
- Are stop/retire items included?
- Is roadmap reviewed regularly?

---

## Summary

Technical strategy turns architecture judgment into a sequence of improvements.

Good roadmaps balance delivery now with capability for the future.
