# Quarterly Engineering Operating Rhythm

## Purpose

This file provides an operating rhythm for engineering leaders to manage architecture, quality, delivery, production readiness, learning, and modernization.

A good rhythm makes governance predictable and lightweight.

---

## Weekly Rhythm

Focus:

- active risks
- production issues
- PR/CI health
- blockers
- dependency status
- quality hot spots
- decision follow-ups

Questions:

- What is blocked?
- What risk changed?
- What failed in CI?
- What incident/action needs follow-up?
- Which decision needs escalation?

---

## Sprint Rhythm

Focus:

- design review
- test strategy
- delivery evidence
- documentation updates
- technical debt slices
- production readiness for upcoming releases

---

## Monthly Rhythm

Focus:

- quality scorecards
- technical debt review
- incident trends
- observability maturity
- security findings
- dependency risks
- documentation freshness
- KT/onboarding feedback

---

## Quarterly Rhythm

Focus:

- technical strategy
- modernization roadmap
- platform standards
- architecture principles
- capability maturity
- team skill growth
- major risk themes
- investment priorities

---

## Suggested Forums

| Forum | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Architecture review | Design decisions and cross-team impacts. |
| Quality review | Scorecards, gates, test maturity, technical debt. |
| Production readiness | Go-live and operational risk review. |
| Incident learning | Post-incident improvement. |
| Platform standards | Reusable patterns, templates, governance. |
| KT forum | Learning, onboarding, domain/technical deep dives. |

---

## Operating Rhythm Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Impact |
|---|---|
| Too many meetings with no output | Governance fatigue. |
| No cadence for quality | Debt grows silently. |
| Strategy reviewed only annually | Roadmap drifts. |
| Incident actions not reviewed | Repeat failures. |
| No owner for forums | Meetings decay. |
| No decision records | Outcomes lost. |

---

## Review Checklist

- Are forums purposeful?
- Are outputs recorded?
- Are actions owned?
- Are decisions documented?
- Are scorecards reviewed?
- Are incidents feeding improvement?
- Are technical debt slices prioritized?
- Is roadmap updated?
- Is learning cadence active?

---

## Summary

Engineering leadership needs rhythm.

Regular lightweight governance prevents last-minute crisis management.
