# Stakeholder Communication, Business Readiness, and Sign-Off

## Purpose

This file explains how to manage stakeholder communication and business readiness during migration.

Migration affects users, business processes, support, risk, and leadership. Communication must be planned.

---

## Stakeholder Groups

Stakeholders may include:

- business owners
- product owners
- operations
- support
- advisors/users
- technology teams
- security/risk
- compliance/legal
- downstream consumers
- regional leads
- leadership

---

## Communication Plan

Define:

- audience
- message
- timing
- channel
- owner
- decision needed
- escalation path
- status cadence
- issue communication
- go-live announcement
- rollback communication

---

## Business Readiness

Business readiness includes:

- user training
- process changes
- access readiness
- report readiness
- support contacts
- known limitations
- fallback process
- go-live date
- hypercare details
- sign-off owner

---

## Sign-Off

Sign-off should be evidence-based.

Sign-off package may include:

- scope
- reconciliation results
- test results
- known issues
- accepted exceptions
- operational readiness
- rollback/fallback plan
- business acceptance
- final go/no-go decision

---

## Communication Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Impact |
|---|---|
| Technical status only | Business unprepared. |
| Risks hidden until cutover | Trust damage. |
| No clear sign-off owner | Decision delay. |
| Known limitations not communicated | User frustration. |
| No rollback communication | Confusion during incident. |
| Too many channels | Message fragmentation. |

---

## Review Checklist

- Are stakeholders identified?
- Is communication plan defined?
- Are business users trained?
- Are known limitations communicated?
- Is support process clear?
- Is sign-off package ready?
- Are accepted exceptions listed?
- Is go/no-go decision owner clear?
- Is rollback communication prepared?

---

## Summary

Migration success requires stakeholder readiness.

A technically successful cutover can still fail if users, support, and decision-makers are not ready.
