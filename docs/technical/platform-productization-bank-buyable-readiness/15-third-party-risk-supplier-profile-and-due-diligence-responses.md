# Third-Party Risk, Supplier Profile, and Due-Diligence Responses

## Purpose

This file explains supplier-side preparation for third-party risk management and due diligence.

Enterprise buyers assess the supplier as well as the product.

---

## Supplier Profile

A supplier profile may include:

- company legal name
- jurisdiction
- ownership structure
- financial stability
- leadership
- key contacts
- insurance
- certifications where available
- support locations
- subcontractors/subprocessors
- business continuity posture

---

## Due-Diligence Topics

Common topics:

- information security
- data protection
- privacy
- operational resilience
- financial stability
- compliance
- audit rights
- business continuity
- incident response
- subcontractors
- concentration risk
- exit plan
- AI governance where applicable

---

## Dependency Disclosure

Disclose:

- cloud provider
- managed services
- critical SaaS tools
- open-source dependencies
- AI/model providers
- monitoring/logging providers
- payment/billing providers where applicable
- support tooling

---

## Response Discipline

Due-diligence answers should be:

- accurate
- evidence-backed
- consistent
- not overclaiming
- reviewed by owner
- versioned
- safe to share
- gap-aware

---

## Due-Diligence Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Impact |
|---|---|
| Inconsistent answers across questionnaires | Buyer trust issue. |
| Overstated certifications | Legal/procurement risk. |
| Dependencies undisclosed | TPRM concern. |
| No BCP/DR answer | Resilience blocker. |
| No insurance posture | Commercial blocker. |
| No evidence for controls | Assessment delay. |
| AI providers not disclosed where relevant | Governance issue. |

---

## Review Checklist

- Is supplier profile ready?
- Are key contacts defined?
- Are certifications/evidence listed accurately?
- Are dependencies disclosed?
- Are subprocessors listed?
- Is BCP/DR posture documented?
- Is incident response documented?
- Is insurance posture known?
- Are answers reviewed?
- Are gaps tracked?

---

## Summary

Third-party risk readiness is part of selling to banks.

A credible supplier can answer risk questions consistently and evidence claims.
