# Business Analyst, Product Technologist, and Domain SME Path

## Purpose

This file provides a learning path for business analysts, product technologists, and domain SMEs.

The goal is to connect business capability, domain meaning, technical design, data, APIs, testing, and evidence.

---

## Core Guides

Read:

1. Enterprise Wealth Management Technology Domain Engineering
2. Enterprise Reporting, Document Generation, Archive, and Evidence Engineering
3. Enterprise API Design, Governance, and Contract Engineering
4. Enterprise Data Products, Data Mesh, Lineage, and Trust Engineering
5. Enterprise Migration, Replatforming, Reconciliation, and Cutover Engineering
6. Enterprise Productization and Bank-Buyable Software Readiness

---

## Skills To Build

| Skill | Practice |
|---|---|
| Capability modelling | Define business capability and ownership. |
| Domain vocabulary | Use precise terms for client, account, portfolio, transaction, performance, risk. |
| API thinking | Understand request, response, errors, pagination, idempotency. |
| Data trust | Ask for source, lineage, freshness, quality, and evidence. |
| Reporting evidence | Understand snapshots, templates, archive, and reproducibility. |
| Migration acceptance | Define reconciliation, tolerances, exceptions, and sign-off. |
| Supported-feature clarity | Separate supported, partial, planned, unsupported. |

---

## BA / Product Questions

For every feature ask:

```text
What business problem does this solve?
Who is the user?
What is the scope?
What data is required?
Who owns that data?
What states are supported?
What happens if data is stale?
What evidence proves output?
What is not supported?
What needs sign-off?
```

---

## Practical Exercises

1. Create a capability-status matrix.
2. Write a glossary for one domain area.
3. Define acceptance criteria with supportability states.
4. Create report evidence requirements.
5. Define migration reconciliation rules.
6. Review API examples for business meaning.
7. Create user-facing limitation notes.
8. Create UAT sign-off evidence checklist.

---

## Summary

Strong BAs and product technologists reduce translation risk.

They connect business truth to technical contracts and evidence.
